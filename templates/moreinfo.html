Image Similarity through Deep Learning

Convolutional neural networks are used for many machine vision tasks; one popular usage is for image classification. GoogleNet, a.k.a. the Inception Network, was developed for the ImageNet challenge where the task was to categorize images according to 1000 given categorical labels. The Inception Network boasts 22 convolutional layers and sets itself apart from other celebrated CNNs by computing some of these layers in parallel modules.

Look-Alike tweaks the already-trained Inception model for the purposes of determining the similarity between dog images. For guessing dog breeds, two additional fully-connected layers were adeed and trained on solely dog images. The process of taking a trained model and repurposing it for another task is known as transfer learning.

However, these breed labels aren't necessarily the best way to represent the finer details of a dog's appearance. In another instance of transfer learning, Look-Alike uses Inception as a feature extractor. The Inception layer immediately before the classification stage represents an image with a 2048-dimensional vector, and the similarity between a pair of images is simply taken as the cosine similarity between their vectors.


In the above plot, images and their respective vectors were taken from some of the most represented breeds on Petfinder. The vectors are reduced from 2048 dimensions down to two using t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding (t-SNE). This embedding ensures that points close to each other are similar in the 2048-dimensional space. Notice that for the most part, different breed dogs can still look similar to each other. Breed tags are limited with regards to describing the look of a dog, which is why the vector representation is used.
